In this talk, I presented our progress in applying machine learning and large language models to battery-material design and literature-driven catalyst screening.

**Key content of the presentation includes:**
* **Integrating AI with battery science:** Discussed how data-driven models can connect materials descriptors, electrochemical conditions, and battery performance.
* **Interpretable machine learning:** Presented data-analysis experience from hard-carbon materials for sodium-ion batteries and coordination-engineered catalysts for lithium–sulfur batteries.
* **Emerging research directions:** Discussed the potential of large language models and closed-loop workflows for scientific information extraction, catalyst evaluation, and materials discovery.
